This project investigates a **low-temperature synthetic route** for preparing **Cu-doped CdTe quantum dots** using clusters as single-source molecular precursors for nucleation.

The central goal is to achieve a more controlled relationship between the precursor cluster and the final quantum dot population while preserving **system homogeneity**, **reproducibility**, and **well-defined dopant incorporation**.

A major part of this work is understanding how Cu behaves inside the material and how that behavior can be probed through complementary characterization techniques such as **XPS**, **EPR**, **EXAFS**, and **magnetic measurements**.
